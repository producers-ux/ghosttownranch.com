No. 01
Origin

Where it
started.

Ghost Town Ranch began in the high desert near Twentynine Palms, California — a stretch of the Mojave where the West is still unfinished and the light comes in flat and honest from the east. It is the kind of place that asks something of you. It does not accommodate. It clarifies.

The property sits on five parcels at the edge of Joshua Tree National Park, where the terrain shifts from creosote flats to granite formations in the space of a mile. The nearest town has a population of sixteen thousand and a marine base at its eastern edge. There are no lifestyle brands out here. There is dirt, sky, and the particular silence of a place that has never been convinced it needs to be anything other than what it is.

That is where the brand was born. Not in a studio. Not at a trade show. On the ground, in the heat, trying to understand what honest personal care actually looks like in a place that has no patience for pretense.

We have since moved our base to Palm Springs, where Desert Modernism gave the world a different answer to the same desert — heat and light resolved into clean lines, glass walls, and the conviction that beauty belongs in dry places. Ghost Town Ranch lives between those two worlds. The ranch and the city. The raw and the refined. The wide open and the life it opens onto.

The desert does not ask you to be comfortable.
It asks you to be honest.

No. 02
Philosophy

Frontier
Modernism.

Frontier Modernism is not a style. It is a disposition. It is what happens when the self-reliance of the pioneer West meets the discipline and restraint of desert modernism — when you believe that objects should earn their place, that materials should be honest, and that the most refined thing you can do is remove everything that does not belong.

The pioneer West was not romantic. It was practical. You built what you needed and left the rest alone. You chose materials that held, tools that worked, and you did not confuse decoration with quality. The frontier had no patience for filler — not in a structure, not in a formula, not in a life.

Desert modernism said the same thing in a different language. Richard Neutra, Albert Frey, the Case Study houses — these were arguments for clarity. For the idea that a building, like a life, should have nothing in it that does not serve. Glass where there was once wall. Sky where there was once ceiling. The desert, invited in.

Ghost Town Ranch is where those two arguments converge. We formulate the way a good architect builds: with intention, without filler, in service of the person who will live inside what we make. Every product is that argument made physical. Pioneer grit. Modern clarity.

No. 03
Landscape

The wide
open.

The desert is a design brief. Wide light, dry air, heat that asks something of every surface it touches. Skin that has to hold without help. Materials that cannot lie about what they are. Scale that makes human concerns feel both smaller and more precise.

We think about this landscape the way a good formulator thinks about an ingredient list: what is essential, what is excess, and what happens when you remove everything that does not belong? The desert answers that question in real time. Anything that cannot sustain itself in this light falls away.

This is not a brand built on nostalgia for the frontier. It is a brand built on what the frontier still teaches, which is that clarity is a form of respect — for the person using the product, for the environment it comes from, and for the standard it is held to. The wide open is not an aesthetic. It is an argument about what matters.

From the wide open, for every day.

No. 04
Standard
The Standard

Not self-declared.
Third-party verified.

Every GTR skincare and body formula is certified under the COSMOS standard, audited by ECOCERT. This is not a marketing claim. It is a third-party verification that every ingredient, every process, and every finished product meets one of the highest natural formulation standards in the world.

COSMOS certification means no parabens, no phthalates, no synthetic dyes. It means that when we say an ingredient comes from organic farming, someone other than us has confirmed it. It means skin-safe fragrance throughout — not because it is easier, but because it is right.

The standard does not change by category. The hand wash holds it. The face serum holds it. The candle wax holds it. We did not build a certified skincare line and a separate uncertified home line. The conviction runs through everything we make.

No. 06
Vision

Where
Frontier Modernism lives.

Frontier Modernism is not a desert category. It is an American West category — and the American West is anywhere the wide open meets a considered life. It is the ski lodge in the pines above Sun Valley where the design is spare and the light is clean. The spa at the edge of a ranch in Utah where the materials are honest and the ritual is the point. The farmhouse in the Hudson Valley where the weekend feels like it was earned.

These are the places Ghost Town Ranch is building toward. Not as a brand that chases markets, but as a world that already belongs in those rooms — because the conviction is the same everywhere the wide open has shaped how people think about what they put on their skin, what they burn in a room, and what they choose to leave on the counter.

The skincare came first, because that is where the standard is hardest to hold and most visible when you do. The scent came next, because a place without a scent is not yet a world. What comes after is the full expression: objects, amenities, and experiences that carry the same material logic into every context where a considered life is being built.

From the wide open. For every day. For everywhere the wide open reaches.
